Alexander McQueen was born on March 17, 1969, in Lewisham, East London, England. He was the youngest of six children in a family where his father worked as a taxi driver and his mother was a social science teacher. From a young age, McQueen showed a keen interest in fashion design, often creating clothes for his three sisters.

At 16, McQueen left school and pursued an apprenticeship at Anderson & Sheppard, a renowned tailor shop on Savile Row in London. He later worked at Gieves & Hawkes and Angels & Bermans, a theatrical costume designer, honing his skills in tailoring and design.

McQueen's career took off after he earned a master's degree in fashion design from Central Saint Martins College in 1992. His graduation collection was bought in its entirety by fashion editor Isabella Blow, marking the beginning of his rise to prominence. In 1996, McQueen was appointed as the chief designer for Givenchy, a prestigious French fashion house. However, due to disagreements with the brand's style, he left in 2001.

In 1992, McQueen also founded his own label, Alexander McQueen, which quickly gained attention for its bold and innovative designs. His brand grew even further when, in 2000, Gucci Group acquired a majority stake in the company, allowing McQueen to continue as creative director.

McQueen's designs are renowned for their dramatic, rebellious nature, often telling a story and incorporating elements of history, nature, and technology. His work was deeply influenced by British history, Gothic culture, natural ecology, and science fiction films. As McQueen once said, "Fashion is a tool to escape reality and a way to express oneself."
